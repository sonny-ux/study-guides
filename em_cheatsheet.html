Electric Field & Charge H&R Ch. 21–22
Definition: $\vec{E} = \vec{F}/q_0$   [N/C = V/m]
Point charge: $\vec{E} = \dfrac{kq}{r^2}\hat{r}$   ($k = 1/4\pi\varepsilon_0$)
Superposition: $\vec{E}_\text{net} = \sum_i \vec{E}_i$   (vector sum over all sources)

Charge distributions:

Linear $\lambda$: $dq = \lambda\,dl$   [C/m]
Surface $\sigma$: $dq = \sigma\,dA$   [C/m²]
Volume $\rho$: $dq = \rho\,dV$   [C/m³]

To find $\vec{E}$: write $d\vec{E} = k\,dq/r^2\,\hat{r}$, then integrate; cancel components by symmetry first.

Electric Flux & Gauss's Law H&R Ch. 23
Flux: $\Phi = \oint \vec{E}\cdot d\vec{A}$   [N·m²/C]
$$\oint \vec{E}\cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{q_\text{enc}}{\varepsilon_0}$$
SymmetryGaussian surface
Spherical (point/sphere)Sphere radius $r$
Cylindrical (line/rod)Coaxial cylinder, $r$, $L$
Planar (sheet/slab)Pillbox area $A$

Key: choose a surface where $E$ is constant and $\parallel d\vec{A}$ (or $\perp$, contributing zero). Then $\oint \vec{E}\cdot d\vec{A} = E\cdot A_\text{eff}$.

Electric Potential H&R Ch. 24
Point charge: $V = kq/r$   (scalar; $V=0$ at $r\to\infty$)
From $\vec{E}$: $\Delta V = V_f - V_i = -\displaystyle\int_i^f \vec{E}\cdot d\vec{s}$
From $V$: $\vec{E} = -\nabla V$   ($\vec{E}$ points from high to low $V$)
Potential energy: $U = qV$   (charge $q$ at potential $V$)
Pair: $U = kq_1 q_2/r$   (work to assemble from $\infty$)

Superposition: $V = \sum_i kq_i/r_i$ — scalar sum, no direction needed. Equipotential surfaces $\perp\,\vec{E}$.

Electric Dipoles H&R Ch. 22–24
Moment: $\vec{p} = q\vec{d}$   (from $-q$ to $+q$; SI: C·m)
Torque: $\vec{\tau} = \vec{p}\times\vec{E}$   (aligns $\vec{p}$ with $\vec{E}$)
Pot. energy: $U = -\vec{p}\cdot\vec{E} = -pE\cos\theta$
Far field: $E_\text{dipole} \propto 1/r^3$   (falls faster than point charge)

Stable equilibrium: $\vec{p}\parallel\vec{E}$ ($\theta=0$, $U=-pE$). Field along axis: $E = kp/z^3$ (far); along perp. bisector: $E = kp/y^3$ (far).

Constants & Key Values
$\varepsilon_0$: $8.85\times10^{-12}$ F/m   (permittivity of free space)
$\mu_0$: $4\pi\times10^{-7}$ T·m/A   (permeability of free space)
$k$: $\dfrac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} = 8.99\times10^{9}$ N·m²/C²
$e$: $1.602\times10^{-19}$ C  |  $c = 3\times10^8$ m/s
$c^2 = \frac{1}{\varepsilon_0\mu_0}$ — Maxwell's relation

$1\,\mathrm{T} = 1\,\mathrm{N}/(\mathrm{A}{\cdot}\mathrm{m})$. Earth's field $\approx 50\,\mu\mathrm{T}$. $1\,\mathrm{G} = 10^{-4}\,\mathrm{T}$.

Unit 5 — Capacitance H&R Ch. 25
Define $C$: $q = CV$   ($C$ in Farads = C/V)
Parallel plate: $C = \dfrac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d}$
Spherical: $C = \dfrac{4\pi\varepsilon_0}{\tfrac{1}{a}-\tfrac{1}{b}}$   (isolated sphere: $C = 4\pi\varepsilon_0 a$)
Cylindrical: $C = \dfrac{2\pi\varepsilon_0 L}{\ln(b/a)}$

Series & Parallel:

Parallel: $C_\text{eq} = C_1 + C_2 + \cdots$   (same $V$, charges add)
Series: $\dfrac{1}{C_\text{eq}} = \dfrac{1}{C_1} + \dfrac{1}{C_2} + \cdots$   (same $q$, voltages add)

Series: $C_\text{eq} <$ smallest. Parallel: $C_\text{eq} >$ largest. Two equal $C$ in series: $C/2$.

Energy stored:

$U$: $\dfrac{q^2}{2C} = \dfrac{1}{2}CV^2 = \dfrac{qV}{2}$
Energy density: $u = \dfrac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2$   [J/m³] — general!

Dielectrics ($\kappa > 1$):

Effect: $C_\kappa = \kappa C_0$   always increases $C$
Fixed $q$: $E\to E/\kappa$, $V\to V/\kappa$, $U\to U/\kappa$ (dielectric pulled in, loses energy)
Fixed $V$: $q\to\kappa q$, $U\to\kappa U$ (battery does extra work)

Partial slab thickness $t$: two capacitors in series — vacuum gap $(d-t)$ and dielectric layer $(t)$. $C = \varepsilon_0 A/(d - t + t/\kappa)$.

Common $\kappa$: Teflon 2.25, Mica 5.4, Si 12, Water 80.

  • 2 Write $EA = q_\text{enc}/\varepsilon_0$; solve for $E$
  • 3 Integrate $E$ to get $V$; use $C = q/V$
Unit 6 — Current & Resistance H&R Ch. 26
Current: $i = dq/dt$   [A = C/s]
Density: $J = i/A$   (uniform); $\;\vec{J} = ne\vec{v}_d$
General: $i = \int \vec{J}\cdot d\vec{A}$   (non-uniform $J$)
Drift speed: $v_d = \dfrac{i}{neA} = \dfrac{J}{ne}$   ($\sim\mu$m/s in Cu)

$n_\text{Cu} = 8.5\times10^{28}$ m$^{-3}$. Electrons drift opposite to $\vec{J}$.

Ohm's Law:

Macro: $V = iR$   [$\Omega$ = V/A]
Micro: $\vec{J} = \sigma\vec{E} = \vec{E}/\rho$   ($\sigma{=}1/\rho$: conductivity)
Wire: $R = \rho L/A$   ($\rho_\text{Cu} \approx 1.72\times10^{-8}\;\Omega\cdot$m)
Temp: $\rho(T) = \rho_0[1+\alpha(T-T_0)]$

$\sigma$ is a material property; $R$ depends on geometry too.

Power:

General: $P = iV = i^2 R = V^2/R$
EMF source: $\mathcal{E} = $ terminal voltage + $ir$   ($r$ = internal resistance)

Real battery: $V_\text{terminal} = \mathcal{E} - ir$. Under load, terminal voltage drops.

Circuit Rules — Kirchhoff H&R Ch. 27
KCL: $\sum i_\text{in} = \sum i_\text{out}$   at any junction (charge conserved)
KVL: $\sum \Delta V = 0$   around any closed loop (energy conserved)

KVL sign convention: traversing resistor against current = $+iR$ drop. EMF source from $-$ to $+$ = $+\mathcal{E}$ gain.

Resistors:

Series: $R_\text{eq} = R_1 + R_2 + \cdots$   (same $i$, voltages add)
Parallel: $\dfrac{1}{R_\text{eq}} = \dfrac{1}{R_1} + \dfrac{1}{R_2} + \cdots$   (same $V$, currents add)

Two resistors parallel: $R_\text{eq} = R_1 R_2/(R_1+R_2)$.

RC Circuits:

Charging: $q(t) = C\mathcal{E}(1 - e^{-t/RC})$   $i(t) = \frac{\mathcal{E}}{R}e^{-t/RC}$
Discharging: $q(t) = q_0 e^{-t/RC}$   $i(t) = \frac{q_0}{RC}e^{-t/RC}$
Time const: $\tau = RC$   (time to reach $1-1/e \approx 63\%$ of final)

At $t = \tau$: capacitor 63% charged. At $t = 5\tau$: essentially fully charged.

  • 1 Identify loops and junctions; assign currents
  • 2 Write KCL at each junction; KVL around each independent loop
  • 3 Solve system; check: more equations than unknowns = redundant loop
Unit 7 — Magnetic Forces H&R Ch. 28
Lorentz: $\vec{F}_B = q\vec{v}\times\vec{B}$   mag. $F = |q|vB\sin\phi$
Wire: $\vec{F} = i\vec{L}\times\vec{B}$   mag. $F = iLB\sin\theta$
Curved wire: In uniform $\vec{B}$: $\vec{F} = i\vec{L}_{ab}\times\vec{B}$ — only endpoints matter

$\vec{F}_B \perp \vec{v}$ always — does NO work. Closed loop in uniform $\vec{B}$: zero net force.

Circular motion ($q$ in $\vec{B}$):

Radius: $r = \dfrac{mv}{|q|B}$   (Larmor radius)
Period: $T = \dfrac{2\pi m}{|q|B}$   independent of speed!
$\omega_c$: $\omega_c = |q|B/m$   (cyclotron frequency)
Accel. $V$: $v = \sqrt{2qV/m}$   (from $qV = \tfrac{1}{2}mv^2$)
Combined: $r = \dfrac{1}{B}\sqrt{\dfrac{2mV}{q}} = \dfrac{1}{B}\sqrt{\dfrac{2V}{q/m}}$

Helical path: $v_\perp = v\sin\phi$ circles, $v_\parallel = v\cos\phi$ drifts; pitch $= v_\parallel T$.

Crossed fields & Hall effect:

Vel. select.: $v = E/B$   (balance $qE = qvB$)
Hall voltage: $V_H = v_d B d$
$e/m$ ratio: $e/m = E/(rB^2)$

Dipole moment & torque:

Moment: $\vec{\mu} = NiA\hat{n}$   (RHR: curl fingers along $i$, thumb = $\hat{n}$)
Torque: $\vec{\tau} = \vec{\mu}\times\vec{B}$   mag. $\tau = NiAB\sin\theta$
Pot. energy: $U = -\vec{\mu}\cdot\vec{B} = -\mu B\cos\theta$

Stable equilibrium: $\vec{\mu}\parallel\vec{B}$ ($\theta=0$, $U=-\mu B$). Unstable: $\theta=180°$.

Gauss's law for magnetism: $\oint\vec{B}\cdot d\vec{A}=0$ — no monopoles, $\vec{B}$ lines always close.

Biot–Savart Law H&R Ch. 29
$$d\vec{B} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi}\frac{i\,d\vec{s}\times\hat{r}}{r^2}$$
Wire segment: $B = \dfrac{\mu_0 i}{4\pi r}\int\sin\theta\,d\ell$
SourceField
Long straight wire$B = \dfrac{\mu_0 i}{2\pi r}$   (circles, RHR)
Circular loop center$B = \dfrac{\mu_0 i}{2R}$   (at center)
$N$-turn coil center$B = \dfrac{\mu_0 Ni}{2R}$
Solenoid (interior)$B = \mu_0 ni = \mu_0 Ni/L$   (uniform)
Toroid (interior)$B = \dfrac{\mu_0 Ni}{2\pi r}$

RHR for wire: thumb along $i$, fingers curl in direction of $\vec{B}$. Parallel wires attract if currents same direction, repel if opposite.

Ampere's Law:

$$\oint\vec{B}\cdot d\vec{s} = \mu_0 i_\text{enc}$$
Use when: high symmetry — straight wire, solenoid, toroid
  • 1 Choose Amperian loop matching symmetry
  • 2 Compute $i_\text{enc}$ through loop surface
  • 3 If $B$ constant on loop: $B(2\pi r) = \mu_0 i_\text{enc}$

Inside a wire of radius $R$ carrying uniform $J$: $B = \frac{\mu_0 i r}{2\pi R^2}$ — grows linearly with $r$. Outside: $B = \mu_0 i/(2\pi r)$ — falls as $1/r$.

Unit 8 — Faraday's Law & Induction H&R Ch. 30
Flux: $\Phi_B = \int\vec{B}\cdot d\vec{A} = BA\cos\theta$ (uniform)
Faraday: $\mathcal{E} = -\dfrac{d\Phi_B}{dt}$   [induced EMF in volts]
$N$ turns: $\mathcal{E} = -N\dfrac{d\Phi_B}{dt}$
Lenz's law: Induced current opposes the change that caused it (negative sign)

Lenz: if $\Phi_B$ increases into page, induced current is CCW (opposes increase).

Motional EMF:

Moving rod: $\mathcal{E} = BLv$   (rod length $L$, velocity $v \perp \vec{B}$)
General: $\mathcal{E} = \oint(\vec{v}\times\vec{B})\cdot d\vec{s}$

Rod on rails in field $B$: $i = BLv/R$, force on rod $= BiL = B^2L^2v/R$ (braking). Power dissipated = $F\cdot v$.

Inductance:

Self-ind.: $\mathcal{E}_L = -L\,di/dt$   ($L$ in Henries = V·s/A)
Solenoid: $L = \mu_0 n^2 V = \mu_0 N^2 A/\ell$
Energy: $U_B = \dfrac{1}{2}Li^2$   (stored in inductor)
$B$ energy density: $u_B = \dfrac{B^2}{2\mu_0}$   [J/m³]
Mutual ind.: $M_{12} = M_{21}$;   $\mathcal{E}_2 = -M\,di_1/dt$

RL Circuit:

Rise: $i(t) = \dfrac{\mathcal{E}}{R}(1 - e^{-t/\tau_L})$
Decay: $i(t) = i_0\,e^{-t/\tau_L}$
Time const.: $\tau_L = L/R$

Inductor opposes changes in current (like inertia). At $t=0$: acts as open circuit. At $t\to\infty$: acts as short circuit.

Maxwell's Equations (Integral Form)
NameEquation
Gauss (E)$\oiint\vec{E}\cdot d\vec{A} = q_\text{enc}/\varepsilon_0$
Gauss (B)$\oiint\vec{B}\cdot d\vec{A} = 0$
Faraday$\oint\vec{E}\cdot d\vec{s} = -d\Phi_B/dt$
Ampere–Maxwell$\oint\vec{B}\cdot d\vec{s} = \mu_0 i_\text{enc} + \mu_0\varepsilon_0\,d\Phi_E/dt$

$\mu_0\varepsilon_0\,d\Phi_E/dt$ = displacement current — needed for consistency when $E$-field changes (e.g., inside a capacitor during charging).

Displacement current:

$i_d$: $i_d = \varepsilon_0\,d\Phi_E/dt$   acts like real current for Ampere's law

Between capacitor plates: $i_d = i$ (same as wire current) — no discontinuity in effective current.

RHR Summary:

  • $q\vec{v}\times\vec{B}$: point $\vec{v}$, curl toward $\vec{B}$, thumb = $\vec{F}$ (flip for $-q$)
  • Wire current: thumb along $i$, fingers curl along $\vec{B}$
  • Loop/coil: curl fingers along $i$, thumb = $\vec{\mu}$ direction
  • Solenoid: RHR for current in coils; thumb = $\vec{B}$ through bore

Parallel wires: same-direction currents attract; opposite repel (two wires define the ampere).
